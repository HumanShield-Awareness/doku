How SentryMail supports awareness requirements from **NIS2** and the **BSI IT-Grundschutz**.

> **Note:** This page is an overview, **not legal advice** and not an automatic proof of "compliance". What is binding is your internal risk analysis, your ISMS and, where applicable, legal counsel.

## NIS2 (EU Directive 2022/2555)

NIS2 requires affected entities to take **risk-management measures**, explicitly including **basic cyber hygiene and cybersecurity training** (Art. 21(2)). Phishing awareness is an established building block of this.

**Platform contribution:**
- Conduct regular, repeatable **awareness campaigns**.
- **Measurability**: open/click/submission rates per campaign and recipient.
- **Evidence**: result evaluation, CSV export and audit log as documentation of the measures carried out.

## BSI IT-Grundschutz

The BSI IT-Grundschutz addresses raising awareness and training in the module **ORP.3 "Awareness and training"**; phishing simulations are a common awareness measure recommended by the BSI.

**Platform contribution:**
- Implementation of practical awareness measures (simulations instead of theory only).
- **Target-group control** via recipient groups (e.g. per department).
- **Repeatability** and progress measurement over time.

## Recommended approach

1. **Define goals** (measure a baseline, improvement over time).
2. **Clarify legal aspects**: involve data protection and, if applicable, works council/staff representation **before** the start; observe data minimization (see [Security](/en/reference/sicherheit/)).
3. **Run regularly** (not one-off) and vary the templates.
4. **Follow up with training** — the simulation is the entry point, not the goal.
5. **Document** (results/export as evidence of the measure).

## Important limits

- No substitute for an **ISMS**, technical protections (mail filters, MFA, EDR …) or a full gap analysis.
- Do **not** use results for individual sanctions — that undermines the awareness culture.
